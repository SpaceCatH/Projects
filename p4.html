My 3D Printing Adventure

Since leaving bswift, I found myself with some extra time to explore new hobbies. One day, my friend Marcus randomly asked whether I wanted to borrow his 3D printer. "Heck yeah!" I said. He dropped off this large, boxy machine that didn't look easy to use. He said I would figure it out.

This thing terrified me at first. Bed height and leveling, extrusion, temperature--all aspects I knew nothing about. I started by downloading someone else's design for a vase. It was really cool watching the printer build the model from bottom-up over the course of 5 or so hours. However, at that point, I noticed it was printing a solid object and not the hollowed version for inserting flowers. That's when I went down a rabbit hole of research into various print settings for a hollowed object. In the end, it was as simple as adjusting the Infill to 0 and thickening the Walls.

The vase turned out great! I only had to stop it before it reached full height since I was heading to Palm Springs for a vacation.

I found that printing pre-designed objects was super easy once I had downloaded the appropriate software. But what I really wanted to learn was how to create my own designs. I found an easy-ish CAD program where I could use various shapes to add/erase, and adjust positioning, angles, and height. It took several days of practice, but I finally go the hang of it.

I started with designing some dice for games. Easy peasy. I next moved onto designing an object to help keep our dryer door from swinging shut and hitting us in the head (very annoying). Works well, but it is a little ugly.

And most recently, I designed this little remote-control holder shaped like a turtle because Laura likes turtles.

I love tinkering around with design and printing objects. It just takes a lot of time, so I have to be very deliberate in my attempts. Overall, it has been a fun journey, and I'm happy to have acquired some new skills!
